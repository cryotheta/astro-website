Exoplanets

Exoplanets are planets, much like the ones orbiting our sun, that we have discovered to be orbiting planets other than our Sun. The first exoplanets were found in the late 20th century (in 1989, 1992, or 1995, depending on ones definition of an exoplanet), and since then we have discovered thousands of these worlds orbiting other stars in the galaxy. We are working on algorithms to detect what type of exoplanet it is and various Machine Learning and Data Proccessing techniques to get relevant information for exoplanet.
